C么te d'Azur - France & Monaco

August 2023

General info

The C么te d'Azur, also known as the French Riviera, is a stunning Mediterranean coastline in southeastern France renowned for its glamorous beaches, vibrant culture, and luxurious resorts. Stretching from Saint-Tropez to the Italian border, it includes iconic destinations like Nice, Cannes, and Antibes, celebrated for their art, history, and upscale lifestyle. Monaco, a sovereign city-state nestled within the C么te d'Azur, is famous for its opulence, the Monte Carlo Casino, and the annual Monaco Grand Prix. Together, these destinations offer a perfect blend of natural beauty, sophistication, and entertainment, making them a magnet for visitors worldwide.

When should you go?

The best time to visit the C么te d'Azur is from late spring to early fall, particularly between May and September. During this period, the weather is warm and sunny, perfect for enjoying the beaches and outdoor activities. The peak summer months of July and August are ideal for vibrant festivals and a lively atmosphere, though they can be crowded and expensive. Additionally, the spring and early autumn months are perfect for events like the Cannes Film Festival in May and the Monaco Yacht Show in September.

Do not miss these places in France

  • Nice: Explore the charming Old Town (Vieux Nice), the Promenade des Anglais, and the Marc Chagall National Museum.
  • Cannes: Famous for its international film festival, enjoy the upscale boutiques and stroll along La Croisette Boulevard.
  • Saint-Tropez: Known for its glitzy beaches, luxury yachts, and a quaint old port full of charm.
  • Antibes: Visit the Picasso Museum and walk along the scenic Cap dAntibes for stunning views.
  • Eze: A medieval hilltop village with panoramic views and the beautiful Jardin Exotique.
  • Menton: Near the Italian border, this "Pearl of France" is known for its citrus groves and colorful architecture.

Things to do in Monaco

  • Monte Carlo Casino: An iconic symbol of luxury, known for its grandeur and high-stakes gaming.
  • Princes Palace: The official residence of Monacos royal family, offering tours and a daily Changing of the Guard ceremony.
  • Oceanographic Museum: A world-class aquarium and museum focused on marine life, built on a cliffside.
  • Monaco Grand Prix Circuit: Even when the race isnt on, you can explore the streets that host this famous Formula 1 event.
  • Monacos Old Town (Le Rocher): Wander its charming streets and visit St. Nicholas Cathedral where Princess Grace is buried.

My experience

Last August, my family and I set off on a week-long road trip through Nice, Cannes, and Monaco, and it was pure magic. In Nice, we loved getting lost in the colorful streets of the Old Town and grabbing gelato before strolling the Promenade des Anglaisits even better with the sea breeze on your face. Driving to Cannes felt like a movie moment, and hanging out on La Croisette made us feel a little fancy (even though we were in flip-flops!). Then came Monaco, which was next-level glamorouswe gawked at the Monte Carlo Casino and couldnt resist driving a bit of the Grand Prix route. Having a car made it easy to explore, and honestly, cruising along the C么te dAzur with those views is half the fun!
