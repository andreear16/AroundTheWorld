Marrakech - Morocco

May 2023

üìçGeneral info

Marrakech, known as the "Red City," is a vibrant destination in Morocco that blends ancient traditions with modern energy. At its heart lies the bustling Jemaa el-Fnaa square, filled with street performers, food stalls, and local artisans. The city's Medina is a maze of narrow alleys, souks, and stunning architecture, including the Koutoubia Mosque and the Bahia Palace. Visitors can also escape to the serene Majorelle Garden or soak in the luxury of traditional hammams. With its rich history, lively atmosphere, and warm hospitality, Marrakech offers a sensory experience like no other.

When should you go?

The best time to visit Marrakech is during the spring (March to May) and autumn (September to November). During these months, the weather is pleasant, with warm days and cool evenings, perfect for exploring the Medina, gardens, and outdoor attractions without the intense summer heat. Winter (December to February) is also a good option, with mild temperatures and fewer crowds, though evenings can get chilly. What should you visit?

  • Jemaa el-Fnaa: The bustling main square of Marrakech, filled with food stalls, performers, and a lively atmosphere.
  • Medina and Souks: Explore the narrow streets and vibrant markets selling everything from spices to handmade crafts.
  • Koutoubia Mosque: An iconic landmark of Marrakech, known for its impressive minaret and historical significance.
  • Bahia Palace: A stunning 19th-century palace showcasing intricate Moroccan architecture and tranquil gardens.
  • Majorelle Garden: A peaceful oasis of exotic plants and vibrant blue buildings, once owned by Yves Saint Laurent.
  • Saadian Tombs: A beautifully preserved royal burial site with ornate decorations and rich history.
  • Agafay Desert: Experience a desert adventure close to Marrakech, with camel rides, quad biking, or glamping under the stars.
